<p><p>The commercial reverse osmosis membrane market primarily features two types: composite membranes and asymmetric membranes. Composite membranes consist of multiple layers, enhancing efficiency and selectivity while maintaining structural integrity, making them ideal for various water purification applications. In contrast, asymmetric membranes possess a single-layer structure with varying pore sizes that facilitate effective filtration by allowing contaminants to be separated from water. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs, including water treatment, food and beverage processing, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
