<p><p>Clad metals are materials composed of two or more metallic layers chemically and physically bonded together, combining the desirable properties of each metal. These materials are widely used in industries such as aerospace, automotive, electronics, and construction due to their improved resistance to corrosion, enhanced thermal and electrical conductivity, and weight reduction. The cladding process allows for cost-effective manufacturing by using less expensive metals as the core while maintaining the performance attributes of more expensive alloys on the surface.</p><p>The Clad Metals Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.2% during the forecast period, driven by the increasing demand for lightweight, high-strength materials in various applications. Emerging technologies in welding and bonding techniques are enabling the production of more complex clad materials, further enhancing their adoption in niche markets. Additionally, the rising focus on sustainable manufacturing processes and recycling of metals contributes to market growth.
