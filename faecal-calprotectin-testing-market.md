<p><p>The fecal calprotectin testing market comprises several diagnostic techniques, including ELISA (Enzyme Linked Immunosorbent Assay), Enzyme Fluoroimmunoassay, and Quantitative Immune-Chromatography. ELISA utilizes enzyme-linked antibodies for sensitive quantification of calprotectin levels in stool samples. Enzyme Fluoroimmunoassay enhances detection through fluorescence, offering rapid results. Quantitative Immune-Chromatography combines immunological and chromatographic methods for straightforward, rapid testing. Each type provides distinct advantages in terms of sensitivity, speed, and ease of use, catering to varying clinical needs for inflammatory bowel disease assessment.</p></p>
