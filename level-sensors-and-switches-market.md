<p><p>Level sensors and switches are critical devices used to monitor liquid and solid levels in various applications. The market is divided into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact sensors directly touch the material, using mechanisms like floats or resistive elements, making them suitable for applications where direct interaction is acceptable. In contrast, non-contact sensors utilize technologies like ultrasonic, radar, or capacitive sensing, allowing measurement without physical contact, ideal for corrosive environments or where contamination prevention is essential.</p></p>
