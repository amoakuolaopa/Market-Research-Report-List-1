<p><ul><li>Internal Fixation System</li><li>External Fixation System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pelvis fixation system market is segmented into two main types: internal fixation systems and external fixation systems. Internal fixation systems are used to stabilize pelvic fractures through implants such as plates, screws, and rods that are placed inside the body, promoting healing with minimal scarring. In contrast, external fixation systems involve stabilization using devices anchored to the bone but positioned outside the skin, often employed in complex fractures or when surgical options are limited. Both systems aim to ensure proper healing and restore mobility.</p></p>
