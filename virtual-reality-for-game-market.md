<p><p>Virtual reality (VR) in the gaming market encompasses several types: augmented reality (AR), mixed reality (MR), and traditional VR. AR overlays digital elements onto the real world, enhancing gameplay through interactive experiences, often using smartphones or AR glasses. Mixed reality combines real and virtual environments, allowing players to interact with both physical and digital elements in real time, creating immersive experiences. Traditional VR immerses users in completely virtual worlds through headsets, offering escape and high levels of interactivity, ideal for deep engagement.</p></p>
